About Me

I am a Computational Science Engineer with expertise across the entire numerical modeling chain, from developing and validating numerical codes to deploying them and using them for complex simulations. I work with both industrial solutions and open-source tools, and I optimize performance through high-performance computing (HPC).

Thanks to my adaptability, curiosity, and strong learning ability, I have gained professional experience in diverse teams, including research labs, engineering offices, and consulting firms. My projects span electromagnetism, solid mechanics, civil hydraulics, and computational fluid dynamics (CFD). These experiences have provided me with a comprehensive understanding of numerical modeling and its applications across scientific and industrial contexts.

Experience

Research Engineer (June 2025 – Present)

Currently working as a Research Engineer at the French National Center for Scientific Research (CNRS), I am part of the Electrical and Electronic Engineering Laboratory (GeePs), where I contribute as a scientific computing engineer.

My work focuses on numerical modeling using dedicated simulation tools such as COMSOL Multiphysics, primarily applied to electromagnetism and multiphysics problems, including electromagnetic compatibility, contactless energy transfer, and non-destructive testing.

I am also involved in the development and industrialization of numerical codes for magnetoelastic constitutive law models. In parallel, I provide computational and numerical support to researchers and PhD students within the laboratory.

This work would not have been possible without my diverse internship experiences, which provided me with multiple perspectives on the field of numerical modeling and scientific computing.

Around numerical models…

Internship in Numerical Method Verification and Validation (June 2022 – July 2022)

During this internship, I contributed to the verification and validation of a numerical method for fluid flow simulations (CFD), based on the Moment-of-Fluid approach, implemented in the open-source code Notus.

My work involved convergence tests, consistency analyses, bibliographic comparisons, and the development of detailed technical documentation to ensure the accuracy, robustness, and reproducibility of the numerical method.

About numerical simulations…

Internship in Numerical Modeling in Marine Hydraulics (June 2023 – August 2023)

During this internship at the Tangier Med Engineering design office, I contributed to the numerical modeling of coastal sediment transport for the Nador West Med port project.

My work involved geospatial and satellite data analysis, frequency processing for pre-processing stages, and the validation of numerical simulations performed using the MIKE 21 hydraulic modeling software.

About scientific software development…

Software Development Internship for FEM Analysis Post-Processing (March 2024 – September 2024)

As part of the R&D team at Capgemini Engineering, I contributed to the development of a post-processing software tool for finite element method (FEM) vibration analysis and real-time signal acquisition systems.

I was also involved in a Digital Twin project focused on anomaly detection using machine learning, bridging numerical modeling, data analysis, and industrial applications.
